# Healer UI Notes

## UI Concepts
- **Clear Role Identification:** The Healer should be immediately recognizable through dedicated UI elements such as a distinct portrait frame and iconography emphasizing restoration (e.g., a green cross or leaf).
- **Spellcasting Interface:** Spell selection is presented in a radial or vertical menu showing available healing spells with icons, cooldown timers, and any required resources.
- **Health Focus:** Highlight allies’ health bars prominently when the Healer is active or selected, enabling quick targeting decisions.
- **Contextual Tooltips:** Provide concise descriptions for each healing ability on hover or selection, including healing amount, range, and any special effects.
- **Minimal Clutter:** The UI should maintain simplicity to avoid overwhelming the player, focusing on essential healing information without excessive overlays.

## Visual Direction
- **Color Palette:** Utilize calm and restorative colors such as greens, light blues, and soft whites to reinforce the Healer’s theme.
- **Iconography:** Use gentle, organic shapes—like leaves, crosses, and hands—to represent healing abilities and state.
- **Animation:** Include subtle glow or pulse effects on healing spells when ready to cast; healing target indicators should be smooth and soothing.
- **Consistency:** Align the Healer’s UI style with overall game aesthetics but differentiate it through softer edges and lighter visual weight.
- **Accessibility:** Ensure all icons and text have strong contrast and scalable size options to maintain readability.

## Player Feedback
- **Spellcasting Feedback:** On spell activation, show a brief animation or particle effect on the casting character and the target to confirm action.
- **Healing Confirmation:** Display numeric healing values floating above affected allies and a brief health bar highlight to confirm impact.
- **Cooldown Indicators:** Clearly show cooldown status through greying out icons and countdown timers.
- **Error States:** Provide immediate visual and auditory signals if a healing spell cannot be cast (e.g., out-of-range or insufficient resources).
- **Targeting Assistance:** Highlight valid targets dynamically as the player selects a healing spell to guide decision-making.
