# Tg Ui Notes

## UI Concepts

- **Isometric Clarity:** Interface layout and visual elements should complement the isometric board perspective, preserving spatial clarity and tactical readability.
- **Modular Panels:** Use modular, context-sensitive UI panels that appear near units or areas of interest to minimize screen clutter while delivering relevant information.
- **Activation Indicator:** Clear, subtle indicators to show which unit is active and available for action, emphasizing the alternating activation mechanic.
- **Minimal Overlay:** Avoid excessive UI overlays on the board; rely on concise iconography and tooltips to communicate unit statuses and action possibilities.
- **Consistent Typography:** Use a clean, legible typeface that scales well across resolutions, prioritizing quick information parsing without distracting from gameplay.

## Visual Direction

- **Color Palette:** Employ a cohesive palette anchored in cool, muted earth tones accented with vibrant highlights for active elements (e.g., blues or purples for active units, warm colors for danger or spells).
- **Iconography:** Design simple, intuitive icons for unit actions, facing direction, and status effects that can be quickly recognized at a glance.
- **Subtle Animations:** Use smooth, restrained animations for transitions (unit activation, spellcasting, damage feedback) to enhance engagement without overwhelming the player.
- **Spellcasting Visuals:** Spell effects should have distinct, stylized visual signatures—glowing runes, particle bursts, or color shifts—that differentiate types of spells and their impact areas.
- **Grid and Tile Styling:** The isometric grid should be lightly visible with clear demarcations for unit movement and attack ranges, supporting tactical positioning without visual noise.

## Player Feedback

- **Spellcasting Feedback:** When a player initiates a spell, highlight target tiles dynamically and provide immediate visual confirmation of effects with animations and contextual messages.
- **Unit Facing and Orientation:** Clearly show unit facing direction using arrows or subtle shadows to support tactical decisions on positioning and attack angles.
- **Turn and Activation States:** Use color changes and subtle pulsing effects on the UI and corresponding units to reinforce whose turn it is and which units remain active.
- **Damage and Status Updates:** Display floating combat text for damage, healing, or status changes (e.g., "Stunned," "Defending"), combined with intuitive icons for quick understanding.
- **Accessibility Readability:** Include contrast checks and scalable text/icons to ensure information is accessible and readable under different lighting conditions or for players with visual impairments.
