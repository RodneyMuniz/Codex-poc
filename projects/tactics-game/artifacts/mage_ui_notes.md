# Mage UI Notes

## UI Concepts
- **Distinct Identity:** The Mage UI features arcane-themed design elements, with mystical symbols and rune-like typography to emphasize the Mage’s magical nature.
- **Minimalist Layout:** Prioritize clear spell slots and cooldown timers to enable quick decision-making without clutter.
- **Intuitive Spellcasting Interface:** Use circular radial menus or click-to-cast buttons to represent spells, enabling fluid selection and casting.
- **Mana Resource Bar:** A prominently displayed mana bar should visually track resource availability, aiding strategic planning.

## Visual Direction
- **Color Palette:** Incorporate deep purples, blues, and silver hues to evoke arcane energy and mystery.
- **Elemental Effects:** Spell icons and UI highlights glow with subtle animated particle effects to represent active magic.
- **Typography:** Use elegant serif or stylized fantasy fonts for Mage titles and spell names, ensuring readability but maintaining thematic consistency.
- **Iconography:** Spell icons are symbolic and distinct, easily recognizable at a glance with simplified shapes combined with magical motifs (e.g., fireballs, frost shards).

## Player Feedback
- **Casting Confirmation:** Visual cues such as a brief glow or aura around the Mage avatar when a spell is cast confirm action success.
- **Cooldown Indicators:** Spells on cooldown show a gradually filling overlay or radial timer to communicate reusability.
- **Miss and Hit Feedback:** Impact effects (e.g., flashes, color changes) inform players of spell success or failure.
- **Resource Warnings:** When mana is low, the mana bar pulses gently red to alert players without distraction.
